Layer 01 · The material

SmartFoam™

The foundational technology. Everything starts here: a proprietary high-density foam engineered to do the job a wooden frame used to do, and to survive being squeezed into a box on the way to your door.

Not foam. SmartFoam™. Ordinary sofa foam is cushioning: it sits on top of a structure someone else built. SmartFoam™ is the structure. It carries load, holds geometry, and returns to its engineered shape after being compressed to a fraction of its size. That single difference is what makes a couch-in-a-box possible, and it is why every other layer of the Lab System™ exists.

The definition

A structural foam, not a comfort layer.

Foam in furniture has always been the soft part. It goes on top of the wood, the metal and the springs, and its only job is to feel nice for a few years until it flattens. The frame underneath does the real work, and the frame is also what makes a sofa heavy, awkward through a doorway, expensive to ship and impossible to compress.

SmartFoam™ was engineered to take that job away from the frame. At 34.8 kg/m³ (ASTM D3574-17) it is dense enough to be structural, and tuned so the density is doing work rather than simply making the sofa hard. It holds the seat height, the back angle and the arm geometry with nothing rigid inside. We call the architecture it enables Boneless Design™: zero-frame construction, where the material and the structure are the same object.

This is also why "memory foam" is the wrong comparison, and why we never use the term for our own material. Memory foam is a slow-recovery cushioning material, typically 50 to 90 kg/m³, designed to sink and mould around a body and stay sunk for a moment. SmartFoam™ is designed to do the opposite: respond, carry and come back, immediately and repeatedly.

How it works

Three behaviours, engineered at the cell.

SmartFoam™ is not one trick. It is three engineered behaviours that have to hold at the same time, in the same material, for a decade.

Behaviour 01

Adaptive Cell Structure

An open-cell architecture: every cell is a tiny structural member with air in it, connected to its neighbours. Open cells let air move through the material instead of trapping it, which is why the foam breathes rather than holding heat the way a closed-cell block does.

Load applied at any point is not absorbed by that point alone. It is distributed across the cells around it, which is what lets a soft-feeling material behave like a structure. Take the load away and the cell walls return the geometry they started with. The cell is the smallest part of the sofa, and every one of them is doing its share.

Behaviour 02

Progressive Support Curve

Comfort and support usually pull against each other: soft foam feels good and gives up, firm foam supports and feels like a bench. SmartFoam™ resolves it by making resistance rise with load instead of staying flat.

Measured by indentation force deflection, the same block reads 231 N at 25% compression, 337 N at 50%, and 466 N at 65%. In practice: soft at first touch, balanced when you sit, firm when you lean all the way back. The material meets you with more the further in you go, which is why it can be both the cushion and the frame.

Behaviour 03

Expansion Memory

A sofa that ships in a box has to survive an insult no traditional sofa is asked to survive: being compressed to a fraction of its volume, held there for weeks in transit, and then asked to be furniture again.

Expansion Memory is the engineered ability to do exactly that. The material takes the compression without the cell walls taking permanent damage, and on release it returns to the shape it was built to hold, not an approximation of it. This is what Compression Technology depends on, and it is the reason a full sectional can arrive as a box one person carries upstairs with no appointment, no tools and no freight truck.
